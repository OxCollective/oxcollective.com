Overview
Beta testing is essential to creating great applications, yet for iOS developers, that was a painful and complicated process. Now one of the main Apple developer tools, TestFlight started with a small team of engineers and designers with a vision: to turn a hack into a free, accessible tool to empower coders. Our designers were there from inception to acquisition, helping turn the platform into a full suite to support app creators through testing, monetization and analytics.

Background

In 2011, mobile apps were an exciting new market. The iPhone blew up and tools like GitHub made it easier than ever to share and connect. Yet, for iOS devs beta testing was challenge. Distributing betas was messy, complicated and required testers to take difficult technical steps.

Created by a small team of developers on a mission to bridge this gap, TestFlight took off quickly and soon thousands of users, including some of the world's biggest brands, were on it.

The Challenge

From 2011-2014, two of the Ox partners were directly responsible for all the design pertaining to TestFlight and all its sub-brands. In that period, these were some of the challenges they encountered along the way.

  • ✰Building a platform that served two very different user groups: developers and testers.
  • ✰Creating a brand that felt exciting but reliable, friendly but professional, accessible but not dumbed-down.
  • ✰Developing a full brand strategy for the original product, and later the suite (after acquisition by Burstly).
  • ✰Understanding the complex technical requirements of developers to build a ground-breaking analytics product

Our process

As a pioneering service, TestFlight had the advantage of filling a market demand and a leg up in terms of adoption—but its success came from more than its great technical aspects. A consistent brand vision and execution with a humanistic tone set the platform apart from most other dev-oriented products at the time.

The UX team, led by Fernando and Björgvin, took a human-centered approach to understand and delight two core user groups (developers and testers) with very different needs; while devs looked for reliability, data and customer service, testers needed ease of use and a friendly interface.

The brand

Starting from the name, creativity and a human face was a high priority. Throghout the life of the project, we paid attention to details, such as color schemes and a carefully crafted and curated iconography. The mascot, a pilot and his plane illustrated by Vanja Mrgan, set the tone and gave the service a human face. The highly requested t-shirts and stickers, as well as pop-up events such as the (fully stickered) taco truck at WWDC inspired trust and generated word-of-mouth—with a sense of community.

Data and analytics, designed for humans

Improving TestFlight's dashboard month after month meant understanding a lot of data, but most importantly the developers that needed it. We interviewed them and discussed creative solutions, tweaking and updating user flows and visual design constantly.

After its acquisition by Burstly, FlightPath, an analytics tool, was introduced. Its mission was to provide actionable analytics. The service was now a full-fledged suite for app developers.

  • ✔Built entirely on human-centered principles
  • ✔Intuitive: instead of tons of options at once, users had a simple click-by-click funnel
  • ✔System was integrated: Analytics tool fed data to our own mobile ad network (SkyRocket)

The results

With overwhelming adoption and widespread praise, TestFlight was first acquired by Burstly—which added FlightPath (analytics) and SkyRocket (monetization), turning the service into a full suite.

In 2014, Apple acquires Burstly. TestFlight is turned into the official platform for iOS development, name unchanged and core brand elements maintained. Many core elements of FlightPath were integrated into iTunes connect.
